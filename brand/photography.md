# Photography Direction

Four distinct art direction categories. Each serves a different brand purpose and carries specific rules.

---

## Category 1: Artful Scientific

**Purpose:** Transforms potentially overwhelming scientific imagery into something contemplative and artful.

**Color Treatment:** Black and white only. Color removed entirely. "By removing color, viewers see the elegant architecture of biology itself."

**Subject Matter:**
- Radial symmetry of microorganisms
- Geometric perfection of bubbles and droplets
- Intricate patterns of cellular structures
- Laboratory glassware
- Microscopic biological forms

**Mood:** Contemplative, mysterious, artful. Not technical and exclusionary.

**Composition:**
- Black backgrounds predominate
- High contrast, dramatic lighting
- Varied scales (microscopic to molecular)
- Close-up, macro-style compositions
- Subjects rendered in white/gray tones against black

**Key Principle:** Abstraction makes complex microbiology accessible. Scientific imagery treated as art objects.

**Example file:** `assets/photography/Artful-Photography.jpg`

## Category 2: Micro Scientific

**Purpose:** Literal scientific compositions that complement the brand's black-and-white photography. Distinct from Artful Scientific - this category INCLUDES selective color.

**Color Treatment:** Color used selectively and purposefully. "Evokes biological complexity without feeling decorative. Palette feels alive but disciplined." Natural biological colors (reds, pinks, yellows, greens, blues, amber).

**Subject Matter:**
- Petri dishes with colored specimens
- Leaves/plant material in glass vessels
- Cellular cultures and colonies
- Lab tools and instruments
- Gloved hands with specimens (intentional human element)
- Test tubes, pipettes, applicators

**Perspective:**
- Top-down perspectives
- Macro perspectives
- Clean, light backgrounds create order
- Generous negative space

**Lighting:** Bright, diffuse, low-shadow. Reveals internal structure and material behavior.

**Human Element:** Intentional inclusion of lab-technician hands wearing dark/black lab gloves. Bridges micro and macro moments. Reinforces human guidance behind personal insights.

**Key Principle:** Light backgrounds (contrast with Artful Scientific's black backgrounds). Order and cleanliness.

**Example file:** `assets/photography/Micro-Scientific.jpg`

## Category 3: Precision Lab

**Purpose:** Automation-led lab photography. Quietly futuristic.

**Subject Matter:**
- Robotic arms and automation equipment
- Sample processing arrays (well plates, microplates)
- Clear/transparent lab equipment
- Tubing, mechanical components, precision instruments
- Server/storage rack environments
- Glass vessels in automated processing lines

**Human Presence:** Minimal to none. Shifts focus to the system itself.

**Composition:** Clean, architectural compositions. Highly controlled lighting.

**Lighting/Color:**
- Industrial yet refined
- Mix of bright white/clinical and dark/dramatic environments
- Cool color temperatures (blues, grays, whites, silvers)
- Crisp detail, subtle reflections highlighting quality and precision

**Mood:** Quietly futuristic. A lab that operates with scale, consistency, and intelligence.

**Key Principle:** Both light (white/bright) and dark (dramatic/moody) environments are valid. Architecture and space as compositional elements.

**Example file:** `assets/photography/Precision-Lab.jpg`

## Category 4: Clinician-Patient Relationship

**Purpose:** Represents the provider-patient relationship as human, attentive, and mediated through advanced science.

**Visual Structure:** Balances two types:
1. Intimate, observational portraits of patients
2. Focused depictions of providers at work

**Patient Photography:**
- Quiet, reflective moments
- Natural lighting
- Tight crops, close framing
- Emphasizes individuality and trust
- Observational, not staged

**Provider Photography:**
- Engaged with tools, samples, and data
- Competent and caring body language
- Professional attire (white coats)
- Active, focused

**Lighting:** Natural light quality for patients. Controlled/directional for providers. Intentionally understated. Reinforces focus and credibility.

**Diversity:** Multiple ethnicities and skin tones. Mix of ages. Both roles shown across demographics.

**Key Principle:** Human connection is central. Warmth and trust balanced with clinical competence. Close framing creates intimacy.

**Example file:** `assets/photography/Clinician-Patient-Relationship.jpg`

## Photography + Data Visualization Overlay

Photography can have data visualization elements composited on top - used sparingly.
- Dark semi-transparent cards overlaid on portrait photography
- Monospaced typography for data labels
- Example: Shannon's Index card (Index Scale: 0-3, Prev Value: 1.2, Ref Range: +2.5, Current Value: 2.3)

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Artful Scientific | Black | B&W only | None | Contemplative, artful |
| Micro Scientific | Light/white | Selective color | Gloved hands | Clean, ordered |
| Precision Lab | Both | Cool tones | Minimal | Futuristic, systematic |

---

## General Rules

- All photography is aspirational and brand-building
- "These guidelines are intended to evolve as our brand does"
- Photography can be full-bleed within layout frames
- Headlines may overlay photography when background allows legibility
- Circular crops used for satellite/ecosystem imagery (orbit diagrams)
